**Zahar Pond** was a noble of [[House Pond]] and the leader of the cadet branch known as _Green Pond_. He was born in the winter of 815 AA and was the firstborn son of **Marvin Pond**.

In 836 AA, when [[House Pond#^MarynPond|Ser Maryn Pond]] and his sons, **Ser Garibald**, **Ser Gelther**, and **Ser Gerwald**, went off to war, Zahar assumed his uncle’s responsibilities alongside his father and governed [[Blue Pond]], [[Appleton]], and [[Village of Grovehaven]]. By altering the tax policies of House Pond, which had favored commoners for centuries, he amassed wealth through private enterprise in Blue Pond until 840 AA, when Ser Maryn returned from _the War of the Flames_.

When his father, **Marvin Pond**, passed away in 851 AA, Zahar inherited his wealth and the responsibility of caring for his siblings. He urged his uncle, Ser Maryn Pond, to name him heir to the house, claiming that his young cousin was not ready to inherit and that he himself had the experience to rule. Enraged by Zahar’s insolence, Ser Maryn Pond ousted him and his siblings from [[Knight's Rest]], declaring that they would trade their family’s knighthood values for gold.

Zahar continued to prepare to press his claim by other means. He married his sister, [[Elena Pond]], to [[Diethard Farkel|Baron Diethard Farkel]] in 856 AA's summer, the liege lord of his family, offering a handsome dowry, and then played a waiting game until his uncle’s death.

After Ser Maryn Pond died in May 857 AA, Zahar challenged the inheritance of his nephew, [[Gregory Pond|Ser Gregory Pond]], claiming that Gregory was not fit to be knighted or to uphold the oaths of their house, and that their hereditary knighthood had ended with Ser Maryn and his three sons. Following this accusation, Baron Diethard Farkel summoned Ser Gregory Pond to answer the charges in a fair trial.
